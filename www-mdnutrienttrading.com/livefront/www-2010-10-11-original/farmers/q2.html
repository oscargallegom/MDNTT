What can I trade?

Once you have determined that you have, or will be able to achieve the baseline requirement for your watershed, you can, take the next step and, determined if you can achieve additional load reductions to sell as credits. Tradable credits can be generated from any planned agronomic, structural, or land conversion practice which you can show will result in additional reductions.

Agronomic practices such as manure injection, reductions in nitrogen fertilizer application, precision agriculture, cover crops, no-till, etc, reduce or minimize surface, groundwater or air emissions of nutrients and credits can be generated from them. They are considered to be annual practices, in that they must be done every year in which credits are needed.

Structural practices such as manure sheds, waterways, fencing etc can generate credits. They can generate credits every year, as long as they are properly maintained.

Land Conversions of agricultural land can be used to generate credits. Several types of agricultural land can be converted to a less nutrient intense land use. Examples include riparian forest buffers, riparian grass buffers, wetlands, and conversion to alternate crops. The Department does not want to encourage the taking of whole or substantial portions of productive farms out of production for the sole purpose of generating nutrient credits. Hence, applications for certification of credits generated in this way will not be approved.
